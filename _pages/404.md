## Brief Bio



I am a postdoctoral research associate in Imperial College London’s [Neural Reckoning group](https://neural-reckoning.org/), advised by Dr Dan Akarca and Dr Dan Goodman. My research sits at the intersection of **temporal learning**, **neuromorphic computing**, and **efficient sequence modelling**. 
A recurring theme in my work is to treat *time* as a first-class computational resource: I study how **learnable delays**, **delay-line memory**, and **lightweight working-memory mechanisms** can expand the computational repertoire of both **spiking** and **non-spiking** neural networks—often with strong constraints on **parameter count, precision, and hardware efficiency**.
I welcome discussions and collaborations on **brain-inspired sequence models**, **efficient AI (LLMs and efficient attention)**, and **neuromorphic architectures**, especially where **algorithm–hardware co-design** and **scalable heterogeneous computation** are central.  
Email: p.sun@imperial.ac.uk

I also co-supervise Master’s students at Imperial, and I’m happy to chat with motivated students interested in NeuroAI and neuromorphic learning.

## Research Themes

My work can be summarised as four connected threads:

- **Temporal parameters as computation (delay learning & time-based degrees of freedom).**  
  I develop learning frameworks where **axonal delays** and **delay heterogeneity** are optimised end-to-end, enabling strong performance on temporally structured tasks (e.g., spiking speech, event-based vision, time-to-first-spike classification), and offering a new axis for model capacity under tight memory/precision constraints.

- **Working-memory mechanisms for efficient sequence modelling (linear memory & delay-line architectures).**  
  I design **delay-line / linear-memory** style modules that enhance the memory capacity of lightweight recurrent and state-space-like models, with applications including speech, biomedical/physiological signals (e.g., ECG), and other long-context temporal streams.

- **Algorithm–hardware co-design for neuromorphic systems and heterogeneous compute.**  
  I study how temporal structure and fast/slow pathways can be exploited jointly by **learning algorithms** and **hardware micro-architectures**, aiming for deployable neuromorphic solutions that are efficient in **memory, latency, and energy**.

- **Biologically grounded efficiency primitives (parameter-free attention & privacy-aware decoding).**  
  I explore **parameter-free attentional mechanisms** inspired by inhibition in SNNs, and develop privacy-aware generative approaches for EEG decoding that reduce user burden while improving robustness.
